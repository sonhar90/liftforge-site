Progress made easy.

LiftForge is a private iOS strength log focused on making progression automatic and obvious. It tracks one working weight per exercise and updates it when you outperform the plan — all on-device, no accounts, no tracking.

Why LiftForge?

Heaviest actual wins

If you planned 80 kg but hit 90 kg on any successful set, next time starts from ≥ 90 kg — works for any exercise ID, including your custom ones.

Standard 5×5 progression

Built-in 5×5-style bumps after successful sessions. If you lift heavier than planned, the heaviest-actual update overrides the automatic bump.

Custom exercises

Create any name. IDs are normalized once, and working weights are stored per ID across sessions.

AMRAP-friendly

Target reps of 0 mean AMRAP; any positive weight counts as a success for progression.

12-week sequencing

A clear A/B cadence expanded over 12 weeks, with an optional “Rest/Bonus” day flow.

Local-first & private

Everything is stored on your device using iOS storage. No analytics. No tracking. No ads.

How it works

  1. Pick a plan. Start with Standard 5×5 or build your own 7-day week (copied across 12 weeks).
  2. Run a guided session. Set the weight, log reps, use the built-in rest timer, and add notes. AMRAP sets use reps=0.
  3. Save — we update. After the session, your working weight for each exercise becomes the higher of (a) the plan’s 5×5 bump or (b) your heaviest successful set. Zero weights are ignored.

FAQ

How are working weights chosen?

One working weight is stored per exercise ID. After a session, a 5×5-style increase applies when you meet targets. If you lifted heavier than planned and hit target reps (or AMRAP), we clamp the new working weight to your heaviest successful set.

Does it work with custom exercises?

Yes. IDs are normalized on create, and weights are keyed by the ID (not the display name).

What data do you collect?

None. LiftForge is local-first and stores everything on your device. See the Privacy Policy.

Is this medical advice?

No. Train responsibly and within your limits.

Which platforms?

Currently iOS only.
